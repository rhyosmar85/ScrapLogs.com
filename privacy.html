Privacy Policy

Effective date: April 21, 2026  ·  Last updated: April 21, 2026

1. Who We Are

ScrapLogs is a SaaS platform built for Florida scrap yards to manage compliance with FL Chapter 538 (Secondary Metals Recyclers and Secondhand Dealers). This Privacy Policy explains what personal data ScrapLogs collects, how it is stored and protected, and your rights with respect to that data.

For privacy inquiries, contact us at privacy@scraplogs.com.

2. Data We Collect

ScrapLogs collects and stores the following categories of personal data as required by FL Chapter 538 and for the operation of the platform:

  • Seller personally identifiable information (PII): full legal name, date of birth, physical address, driver's license number and issuing state, physical description (distinguishing features), and government-issued ID scan image.
  • Biometric data: fingerprint images captured at the point of transaction per FL §538 seller verification requirements.
  • Seller photographs: digital photos captured at the time of each transaction.
  • Signed sworn statements: scanned or digitally captured seller attestations.
  • Transaction records: material descriptions, weights, prices paid, hold period data, vehicle and trailer information, and payment method details.
  • Account data: yard staff email addresses, usernames, and role assignments.
  • Usage data: application logs, login events, and audit trail data.

3. How We Store Your Data

All ScrapLogs customer data is stored on Microsoft Azure infrastructure located in the United States:

  • Azure SQL Database: transaction records, seller records, and structured application data. Each ScrapLogs customer (yard) receives a completely isolated database — your data is never co-mingled in shared tables with another yard's data.
  • Azure Blob Storage: ID scan images, seller photographs, fingerprint images, and sworn statement documents. Each yard's files are stored in an isolated storage container.
  • Encryption at rest: All stored data — including biometrics and PII — is encrypted using AES-256. Azure-managed keys are rotated automatically.
  • Encryption in transit: All data transmitted between the ScrapLogs application and its servers is encrypted using TLS 1.3. Unencrypted HTTP connections are not accepted.

4. Retention Period

FL §538.09 requires that all transaction records be retained for a minimum of three (3) years. ScrapLogs enforces this requirement automatically:

  • Transaction records, seller records, and associated documents are retained for a minimum of 3 years from the date of the transaction.
  • No records within the retention window are deleted, whether through user action or system automation.
  • After the retention window closes, data may be purged upon written request from the yard operator, subject to any outstanding law enforcement holds or legal obligations.

5. Who Has Access to Your Data

Access to data stored in ScrapLogs is restricted as follows:

  • Yard staff: Users authorized by the yard's account administrator may access records for their yard only. Staff cannot access records belonging to another yard.
  • Law enforcement: Per FL §538.19, scrap yards are required to submit daily transaction reports to local law enforcement. ScrapLogs generates and transmits these reports automatically. Per FL §538.19(1), price data is excluded from law enforcement copies. Yard operators may also produce records in response to lawful requests. ScrapLogs does not independently share records with law enforcement beyond what the yard operator authorizes or what is required by statute.
  • ScrapLogs staff: Authorized ScrapLogs engineering and support staff may access data for the purpose of platform operation, incident response, and customer support. Access is logged and restricted to the minimum necessary.
  • Third parties: ScrapLogs does not sell, rent, or share customer data with third parties for marketing or commercial purposes. Sub-processors (e.g., Microsoft Azure) are bound by contractual data protection obligations consistent with this policy.

6. Breach Notification

ScrapLogs complies with the Florida Information Protection Act (FIPA), Fla. Stat. § 501.171. In the event of a data breach involving personal information:

  • ScrapLogs will notify affected customers within 10 business days of determining that a breach has occurred, or within any shorter timeframe required by applicable law.
  • Notification will include the nature of the breach, the categories of data involved, the steps ScrapLogs has taken to contain the breach, and recommended actions for affected individuals.
  • Where required, ScrapLogs will notify the Florida Department of Legal Affairs if the breach affects 500 or more Florida residents.

7. Your Rights

Yard operators and their authorized staff may request:

  • A copy of all data stored for their yard (data export).
  • Correction of inaccurate account or configuration data.
  • Deletion of data that is no longer within the mandatory FL §538 retention window.

To exercise these rights, contact privacy@scraplogs.com.

8. Changes to This Policy

ScrapLogs may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be communicated to account administrators by email at least 14 days before taking effect. Continued use of the platform after the effective date constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
