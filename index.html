Florida Law — FL Chapter 538

Florida law requires every scrap yard to submit a transaction report to local law enforcement by 10AM the next business day.

A single missed report = fines per occurrence. Missing seller records = forced closure.

ScrapLogs automates every deadline automatically — so you never face an audit unprepared.

Fine exposure: $1,000/day — up to $500,000 per breach (FL FIPA)

FL Chapter 538 — Built In

11 Rules. Automatically Enforced. Zero Manual Work.

Every FL Chapter 538 requirement built into the workflow — not a checklist you manage yourself.

FL §538.09

Record Retention

All transaction records retained for 3 years automatically. Never manually archived or deleted.

FL §538.19(1)

Electronic Report to Law Enforcement

Daily LE report auto-generated at 6AM. Transmitted before 10AM deadline. Price data excluded from LE version per statute.

FL §538.19(2)(h)(4)

Vehicle & Trailer Tag Required

Vehicle make, model, license plate AND trailer tag captured on every ticket. Both fields required.

FL §538.23(3)

Hold Period Enforcement

Restricted materials held automatically. Payment release blocked until hold clears. Copper: 7 days. Catalytic converters: 7 days. AC coils: 7 days. Utility wire: 5 days.

FL §538.235(1)

Cash Payment Cap ($1,000)

Cash transactions over $1,000 are automatically blocked. System enforces check or ACH with statute error message.

FL §538.235(2)

Check Must Be Mailed to Street Address

Restricted material payments by check trigger mandatory mail enforcement. PO Box addresses are rejected by the system.

FL §538.26(5)

Proof of Ownership for Restricted Metals

Proof of ownership or authorization required before any restricted metal can be added to a ticket. 90-day validation enforced server-side for authorization letters.

FL §538

Purchasing Hours (7AM–7PM)

Ticket creation is blocked outside 7AM–7PM. System returns statute reference on rejection.

FL §538

Seller Must Be 18 or Older

Age validated from date of birth at customer creation. Ticket open blocked for minors with statute reference.

FL §538

Seller Identification (4 Required Captures)

ID scan, seller photo, fingerprint, and signed sworn statement all required. Ticket cannot be closed until all four are captured.

FL §538

Distinguishing Features Required

Physical description of seller is a required field. Customer record cannot be created without it.

11 rules across 7 statute sections. All enforced server-side. No client-only workarounds.
